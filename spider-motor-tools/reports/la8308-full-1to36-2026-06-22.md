# LA8308 — full 1:36 (two-stage) gearbox free-spin (2026-06-22)

Replacement motor (Eaglepower LA8308 KV90, 20 pole-pairs) connected through the
**complete two-stage 1:36 gearbox**, output free. Mirrors the DIY motor #10
full-gearbox retest (`motor-10-full-gearbox-40v-2026-06-20.md`) so the two
motors are directly comparable on the same mechanism.

- ODrive serial 3482345a3034, vbus 40.0 V, axis0 AS5047P (mode 257), Kt 0.081,
  2 Ω brake resistor armed (`max_regen_current=0` → regen dumped to resistor).
- Commutation: fresh **parked** offset cal first (elec 40.5°, spread 0, drift
  −1.3° → mount solid) — important, since the DIY "binding" was really a bad
  offset, not the gearbox.
- Tool: `tools/la8308_characterize_tune.py --characterize --spin-soak`
  (free-spin sweep + back-EMF fit, then 60 s sustained soak). Current cap 12 A.
- Report: `la8308-full-1to36-2026-06-22.json`.

## Result: the full 1:36 drivetrain is HEALTHY (no binding, no slip)

Free-spin, mean / max |Iq| (motor-shaft turns/s):

| cmd t/s | LA8308 + 1:36 | DIY #10 + 1:36 (offset pinned) | DIY 36:1 (bad offset) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 5  | 1.61 A / 3.03 A → 5.01 t/s ✅ | 1.36 A / 1.82 A → 5.14 ✅ | 3.21 A → 3.35 |
| 10 | 1.78 A / 2.81 A → 9.99 t/s ✅ | 1.50 A / 2.43 A → 10.23 ✅ | 13.07 A → **STALLED** |
| 15 | 1.89 A / 4.52 A → 14.95 t/s ✅ | 1.43 A / 2.39 A → 15.24 ✅ | — |
| 20 | 2.27 A / 4.88 A → 19.93 t/s ✅ | 1.46 A / 2.69 A → 20.37 ✅ | — |

- Flat ~1.6–2.3 A across the whole range, reaches full commanded speed to
  20 t/s, peaks stay <5 A. Nothing like the bad-offset DIY 36:1 stall (13 A at
  10 t/s) — and we now know that stall was commutation, not the gearbox.
- **Back-EMF fit:** λ = 2.677 mWb, Kt = 0.0803 Nm/A, Kv = 103.0 RPM/V — matches
  the stored / bare-motor fit, so the full gearbox does not perturb the motor
  constants; clean measurement.
- **60 s soak** (15 s each at 5/10/15/20 t/s): no slip, vel tracked, |Iq| mean
  1.55–2.04 A, FET 37 → 38 °C. Offset drift across the full-gearbox load event
  only **−0.66°** → magnet holds under the higher reflected torque too.

## LA8308 vs DIY — fair comparison in torque, not amps

The LA8308 draws *more amps* (1.6–2.3 vs 1.4–1.5) only because its Kt is ~2.4×
smaller (0.081 vs DIY 0.194 Nm/A). Converting mean current to motor-shaft torque:

| cmd t/s | LA8308 drag | DIY #10 drag |
|---|---|---|
| 5  | ~0.130 Nm | ~0.264 Nm |
| 10 | ~0.144 Nm | ~0.291 Nm |
| 15 | ~0.153 Nm | ~0.277 Nm |
| 20 | ~0.184 Nm | ~0.283 Nm |

- Same gearbox, so the gearbox drag torque is common to both; the difference is
  the **motor's own loss**. The LA8308 drivetrain drags ~0.13–0.18 Nm vs the
  DIY's ~0.28 Nm — roughly **half the total drag torque**, consistent with the
  LA8308 being a much lower-loss motor (~0.04 Nm vs ~0.4 Nm bare no-load).
- Even the worst-case current peaks are lower in torque (LA8308 0.40 Nm @20 t/s
  vs DIY 0.52 Nm).

## Conclusion

The LA8308 through the full 1:36 gearbox is healthy and smooth — reaches 20 t/s
(~200 °/s at the joint) at ≤2.3 A, no binding, no magnet slip under the load
event. It is a lower-loss drivetrain than the DIY #10 it replaces (~half the
drag torque), and the re-bonded magnet now holds through the full reflected
torque. Next: tune gains with the full gearbox attached and run a loaded hold
test (watch the continuous-current duty limit from the motor-#10 thermal
failure — geared gravity-hold is continuous-current duty).

All config RAM-only; nothing written to flash.
