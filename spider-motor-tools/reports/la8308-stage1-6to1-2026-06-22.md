# LA8308 — first-stage (1:6) gearbox free-spin (2026-06-22)

Replacement motor (Eaglepower LA8308 KV90, 20 pole-pairs) connected through the
**first 1:6 reduction stage**, output free. Mirrors the DIY motor #10 stage-1
test (`motor-10-stage1-6to1-2026-06-20.md`) so the two motors are directly
comparable on the same mechanism.

- ODrive serial 3482345a3034, vbus ~39.9 V, axis0 AS5047P (mode 257), Kt 0.081.
- Tool: `tools/la8308_characterize_tune.py --characterize` (free-spin sweep +
  back-EMF fit; RAM-only, nothing written to flash).
- Report: `la8308-stage1-6to1-characterize-2026-06-22.json`.

## Result: first 1:6 stage is HEALTHY (no binding)

Free-spin, mean / max |Iq| (motor-shaft turns/s):

| cmd t/s | LA8308 + 1:6 | LA8308 bare | DIY #10 + 1:6 | gearbox drag (Δ vs bare) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 5  | 1.88 A / 3.81 A → 5.04 t/s ✅ | 0.50 A | 1.45 A | +1.38 A |
| 10 | 1.83 A / 2.68 A → 10.00 t/s ✅ | 0.44 A | 1.26 A | +1.39 A |
| 15 | 1.97 A / 4.29 A → 14.98 t/s ✅ | 0.47 A | 1.32 A | +1.50 A |
| 20 | 2.08 A / 3.88 A → 20.01 t/s ✅ | 0.67 A | 1.44 A* | +1.41 A |

\*DIY 20 t/s was bus-voltage-limited at 30.8 V (reached only ~15.9 t/s). The
LA8308 at 39.9 V reached a true 20.0 t/s — no voltage ceiling in this sweep.

- Drag current is **flat at ~1.4 A across the whole range** (Coulomb friction,
  speed-independent), peaks stay <4.3 A, and every commanded speed is reached
  cleanly. Same signature as the healthy DIY first stage; nothing like the 36:1
  binding failure (13 A stall at 10 t/s).
- **Back-EMF fit:** λ = 2.705 mWb, Kt = 0.0812 Nm/A, Kv = 101.9 RPM/V —
  identical to the bare-motor fit (0.0810 / 102), confirming the gearbox does
  not perturb the motor constants and the measurement is clean.

## LA8308 vs DIY — fair comparison in torque, not amps

The LA8308 draws *more amps* for the same job only because its Kt is ~2.4×
smaller (0.081 vs DIY measured 0.194 Nm/A). Converting current to motor-shaft
torque:

| | bare no-load drag | + 1:6 stage total | gearbox drag torque |
|---|---|---|---|
| **LA8308** | ~0.036 Nm (0.44 A) | ~0.148 Nm (1.83 A) | ~0.11 Nm |
| **DIY #10** | ~0.409 Nm (2.11 A) | ~0.244 Nm (1.26 A) | ~0.10 Nm |

- Gearbox drag torque is essentially the same (~0.10–0.11 Nm) — same mechanism.
- The **LA8308 motor itself is dramatically lower-loss**: ~0.04 Nm no-load drag
  vs the DIY's ~0.4 Nm. Much smoother, far less cogging/iron loss — better for
  the joint.

## Conclusion

The first 1:6 stage with the LA8308 is healthy and smooth. Expect ~1.4 A
(~0.11 Nm motor-shaft) of constant gearbox drag to add on top of any load
current. Next: tune controller gains with the stage attached
(`--tune`), and/or fit the second stage and re-run this sweep.
